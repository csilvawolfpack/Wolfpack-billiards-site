Our Story

We Started Roaming.
We Found Family.

Wolfpack started in 2024 as a travel tournament team. Mike built it on one idea: family beats ranking. Players who showed up for each other before they showed up for themselves. Two state championships later, the team was something bigger than a roster.

You're never alone on a table when you have the whole Pack behind you.

So in 2026, Mike and Colin signed the papers and turned the Pack into a brand. Same family. Same idea. Built now to bring more players in, make gear by players for players, and give 10% of every sale back to the community that built this thing in the first place.

Meet The Pack

The Founders

The day Wolfpack Billiards became a company. Sharky's, May 2026.

Mike Orr
Founder

Mike Orr's pool story starts in 2015 with a phone call from a friend: "I need another low-rank player on the team." He showed up. He fell in love. Not with winning — with the room. The atmosphere. The people. The strange family that forms around a pool table when nobody's in a hurry to leave.

He's the player, the recruiter, the connector — the one who knows which Pack member is having a rough month and checks in. He runs the room.

Right beside him through all of it: his wife Jenn — known to the Pack as "J-Lo" — captain of her own winning tournament teams. The "family" in "family beats roster" isn't a slogan. It's how Mike and J-Lo actually live.

"There's always room to run with the pack."
Colin Silva
Co-Founder

Colin Silva and his friends were just broke kids who wanted to play pool. They figured out — the way broke kids in every pool hall figure it out — that if you stuffed paper towels into the pockets of a quarter table, the balls would stay up and you could play longer. That's where his game came from. No coach. No lessons. Just hours at tables he couldn't afford, learning by losing to people twice his age.

Twenty years in, he still plays five nights a week at Sharky's Bar Grill and Games in DFW, where he knows every league player by first name.

He helps lead the brand, design, and operations — the parts of the business that have to work whether anyone's watching or not.

Right beside him: his girlfriend Rosie — two states on the road with the team and now a full-fledged Pack member. The family keeps growing.

The Give-Back Program

Run With The Pack.
We Give It Back.

10%
Of Every Sale Goes Back

Every shirt. Every glove. Every piece of chalk.

10% of every Wolfpack sale goes into a community give-back fund — tracked publicly, voted on by the Pack at each tournament. The money goes to one of three places:

  • League Fees & Player SponsorshipsFor players who can't afford to play
  • Youth Billiards ProgramsBuilding the next generation of the game
  • Tournament Prize PoolsSupporting community events
You Buy. The Pack Votes. We Post The Receipts.

The pool community gave us a place to belong. We're not interested in selling them gear without giving something back.
